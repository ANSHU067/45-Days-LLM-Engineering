The 10-Second
Profile Test

A recruiter spends seconds on your profile. Make those seconds count — headline, photo, bio.

Yesterday you picked a platform. Today we make it pass the glance test.
The hard truth

You get ~10 seconds

Recruiters and clients skim dozens of profiles. They don't read — they scan. In about 10 seconds they decide: interesting, or next?

So your profile's job isn't to tell your life story. It's to pass one quick glance.

What they actually look at

The 3 things scanned first

🖼️ Photo

Clear face, friendly, looks like a professional. Not a blurry selfie or empty avatar.

✍️ Headline

The one line under your name. The single most important real estate you own.

📋 Bio / About

The first 2–3 lines. The rest is rarely read on the first pass.

The formula

Your headline = Role + Niche + Proof

Don't write "Student" or "Aspiring something." Tell them what you do and for whom.

# pattern
[What you build] + [for whom / with what] + [signal]

"Aspiring" and "fresher" shrink you. Lead with the work, not the apology.

Weak → strong

Headline makeovers

✗ "B.Tech student | Passionate about coding"
✓ "I build LLM apps with Python & Gemini · learning in public"

✗ "Aspiring AI/ML enthusiast"
✓ "Building RAG chatbots · sharing my 45-day AI journey"
The About section

A 3-line bio that works

  • Line 1 — what you do / are building. "I build AI apps that summarize and answer questions over documents."
  • Line 2 — proof / journey. "Currently completing a 45-day LLM engineering program, shipping 4 deployed projects."
  • Line 3 — a hook / CTA. "Sharing what I learn daily — follow along or say hi."
Don't trip on the basics

Photo, banner & handle

✅ Do

Clear headshot · readable name · a simple banner that states your focus · same handle/photo everywhere.

❌ Avoid

Group photos · default avatars · jokey handles · mismatched names across platforms (you become un-findable).

Be findable

Consistency = discoverability

Use the same name, handle, and photo across LinkedIn, GitHub, and X. When someone hears about you in one place, they should find you instantly everywhere else.

Add your niche keywords (e.g. "RAG", "LLM") to your headline so search surfaces you.
