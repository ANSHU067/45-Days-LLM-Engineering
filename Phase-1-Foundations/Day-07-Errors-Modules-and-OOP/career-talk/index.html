Flip the script

"I'm just a beginner" is a superpower

You think your lack of experience is a weakness to hide. Online, it's the opposite.

Beginners are relatable, easy to root for, and perfectly placed to teach the people one step behind them. Experts forget what it's like to be confused. You don't.

What people connect with

Stories > credentials

📜 Credentials

"3.2 LPA expected, knows Python, OOP." Forgettable. Sounds like everyone.

📖 A story

"BCA student from a small town, learning to build AI in public over 45 days." Memorable. People follow journeys.

The learn-in-public edge

Teach what you learned yesterday

You don't need to be ahead of everyone — just one step ahead of your reader. The Python tip you got today is gold to someone starting tomorrow.

This removes the "who am I to post?" excuse for good.

The framework

Where from → where going → why

  • Where you're from: your background, honestly. (Student, switcher, small town, non-CS — all great.)
  • Where you're going: what you're building toward (AI engineer / freelancer).
  • Why now: what made you start, and what you're committing to publicly.
Structure any post like this

Before → After → Bridge

  • Before: where you were (confused, curious, stuck).
  • After: where you're headed (the goal, the commitment).
  • Bridge: the plan that connects them (this 45-day journey) + an invite to follow.
A skeleton to steal

Your intro post

// hook
I'm starting a 45-day journey to become an AI engineer — in public.

// where from + why
I'm a [background]. I've always [curiosity], and AI is where I want to build.

// the commitment
For the next 45 days I'll share what I build & learn — projects, wins, and bugs.

// invite
Follow along if you're into AI or starting your own journey. 🚀
Keep it human

A few quick rules

  • Write like you talk. No corporate stiffness.
  • Be specific and honest — vulnerability builds trust.
  • End with a small invitation ("follow along", "say hi").
  • Done > perfect. You can always post better tomorrow.
